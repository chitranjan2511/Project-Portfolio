# South Asia Macroeconomic Stability Index Model

## Project Overview

This project develops a composite macroeconomic index to evaluate the industrial sector performance and economic stability of South Asian countries.

The model compares countries based on multiple macroeconomic indicators to assess industrial competitiveness and investment readiness.

## Objective

To construct a quantitative framework that ranks South Asian economies based on industrial growth potential and macroeconomic stability.

## Variables Used

The model incorporates 11 macroeconomic indicators:

* GDP growth rate
* Inflation rate
* Gross domestic savings
* Age dependency ratio
* Control of corruption index
* Employment in industry
* Industry value-added growth
* Political stability index
* Access to electricity
* Gross capital formation
* Population density

## Methodology

Step 1: Data Normalization
Min–Max scaling used to standardize variables.

Step 2: Sub-index Construction
Each variable is multiplied by an assigned weight.

Step 3: Composite Index Calculation
Weighted values aggregated to generate a final country score.

## Data Sources

International Monetary Fund (IMF)

## Key Findings

India ranked highest due to strong industrial growth and investment momentum.

Maldives and Bhutan performed relatively well due to infrastructure and stability.

Bangladesh showed strong industrial employment growth but weaker institutional factors.

Sri Lanka, Nepal, and Pakistan ranked lower due to macroeconomic instability.
